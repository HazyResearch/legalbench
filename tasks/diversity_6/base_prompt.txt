Diversity jurisdiction exists when there is (1) complete diversity between plaintiffs and defendants, and (2) the amount-in-controversy (AiC) is greater than $75k.

Q: Oliver is from Oklahoma. Sophia is from Nevada. James is from Tennessee. James is from Pennsylvania. Oliver and Sophia both sue James for trademark infringement for $70,000 and securities fraud for $17,000. Oliver and Sophia both sue Amelia for legal malpractice for $21,000 and medical malpractice for $69,000. Is there diversity jurisdiction?
A: Yes

Q: Henry is from Massachusetts. Mia is from South Dakota. Olivia is from Colorado. Olivia is from South Dakota. Henry and Mia both sue Olivia for securities fraud for $71,000 and trespass for $76,000. Henry and Mia both sue Liam for defamation for $58,000 and breach of contract for $61,000. Is there diversity jurisdiction?
A: No
